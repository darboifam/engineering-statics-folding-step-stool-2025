# Folding Step Stool – Engineering Statics Final Project

This repository contains our Engineering Statics final project: the design and analysis of a folding step stool. The project focuses on creating a safe, stable, and portable stool and supporting the design with statics-based engineering analysis.

## Project Overview
The goal of this project was to design a folding step stool and justify the final design using:
- free-body diagrams
- equilibrium equations
- tipping analysis
- material selection
- safety factor evaluation

## Final Design Summary
Our final design is a folding step stool with a square top platform, four legs, brackets, rubber grips, and bolted connections. The stool was designed to be compact when folded while remaining stable under load.

### Key Dimensions
- Platform size: 23 in × 23 in
- Height: 12.17 in
- Footprint: 23.465 in × 23.465 in
- Folded thickness: 4.5 in
- Bolt diameter: 0.5 in

## Materials
- **Top platform:** 6061-T6 aluminum plate
- **Legs:** 6061-T6 aluminum rectangular tube
- **Brackets:** A36 steel plate

These materials were selected to balance stiffness, strength, durability, and weight.

## Engineering Analysis
The project includes:
- free-body diagrams for center and edge loading cases
- equilibrium analysis using ΣFx = 0, ΣFy = 0, and ΣM = 0
- tipping stability analysis
- component and connection safety factor checks

Our analysis showed that the stool satisfies the required minimum safety factor of 3, and the final presentation discusses a more conservative overall safety approach for real-world loading conditions.

## Tipping Check
For the edge-loading case:
- Actual loading distance, **B = 11.5 in**
- Distance to tipping boundary, **x = 11.7325 in**

Since **B < x**, the design does **not** tip under the analyzed worst-case loading scenario.

## Weight Estimate
Estimated total stool weight:
- Top platform: 31.0 lb
- Legs: 6.6 lb
- Brackets: 8.8 lb

**Net stool weight: ~46 lb**

## Target Application
Although the original assignment focused on small-space use, our final concept is better suited for factory and warehouse environments because of the final weight and robust construction.
